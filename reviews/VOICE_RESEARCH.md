# VOICE_RESEARCH — GUARDIAN demo narration

**Project:** Google for Startups AI Agents Challenge, Track 3 (Marketplace + Gemini Enterprise)
**Deliverable:** 3-min video, ~2:30 of narration across 12 segments
**Audience:** Google engineers + product leaders + Google Cloud sales execs
**Date:** 2026-05-16

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE PROFILE

**The voice GUARDIAN needs is a calm, mid-low-pitched American male baritone in his 30s–40s, speaking at 145–160 WPM with high prosodic variation, pre-claim pauses of 0.6–0.9s, and zero vocal fry or uptalk.** Research is unambiguous on three of these dimensions and nuanced on the fourth (gender).

Hard parameters:
- **F0 (pitch):** target speaker baseline 95–125 Hz. Klofstad's election studies and the 2025 Frontiers systematic review converge on "lower is more competent" in *public communication* contexts even though some private-comms studies favor higher pitch. Enterprise demos are public communication.
- **WPM:** 145–160 sustained. Drop to ~95–110 WPM for the opening hook and the KPMG name-drop ("180-word intro at 90 WPM," Jobs-style). Faster than 180 WPM costs 17–25% comprehension.
- **Prosody:** high pitch variation (the Anderson/Klofstad-adjacent literature ties pitch variety directly to perceived competence, character, and charisma). Monotone is the single most cited credibility killer.
- **Gender call:** male — but the *reason* is hedged. Research is mixed on gender per se; what's robust is that low-F0 voices win on competence/authority in public-leadership framings. Male baritones land in that F0 band more naturally than most female voices, which is why enterprise B2B tech (Apple, Google Cloud, AWS) defaults male. A low-mid-pitched female baritone-style voice (e.g., ~165–180 Hz, no fry) is a defensible second choice. We will NOT pick a high-pitched female voice (e.g., Charlotte at 218 Hz, Sarah-AsyncAI at 278 Hz) regardless of how "premium" it sounds.
- **Accent:** General American, no regional color. British (received pronunciation) tests slightly higher on perceived credibility/refinement in some lab studies, but Google Cloud's judges are US-based product/sales execs and accent-mismatch raises cognitive load. Neutral GA is the safe pick.
- **Vibe descriptor for ElevenLabs filtering:** "deep, calm, confident, professional, documentary, news anchor — not gravelly, not breathy, not overly warm."

**One-line spec:** *General American male baritone, ~110 Hz F0, 150 WPM, documentary-news register, high pitch variation, deliberate pauses.*

## 2. EVIDENCE

### 2.1 F0 / pitch for persuasion and authority

**Klofstad et al. — Sounds like a winner (2012):** Both men and women select male and female leaders with lower voices. Recordings of "I urge you to vote for me this November" were digitally pitched up/down (~40 Hz delta — roughly A3 to middle C). Both male and female listeners chose the lower-pitched candidate at statistically significant rates.
[PMC link](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3350713/) · [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22418254/)

**Klofstad — Candidate Voice Pitch Influences Election Outcomes (Political Psychology, 2016):** Analysis of all 435 U.S. House elections in 2012 showed candidates with lower voices won a larger vote share against same-sex opponents. Mechanism: lower pitch perceived as greater physical prowess, integrity, strength, and competence.
[PDF — Klofstad](https://web.as.miami.edu/personal/cklofstad/25_polpsych_pitch.pdf) · [Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/pops.12280)

**2025 Frontiers systematic review** (Tigue et al., 24 studies 2012–2024 across EU/US/Asia): "Pitch seems to be influential when assessed in combination with other acoustic features, while as a sole factor it appears to be less reliable." Key directional finding: *"In public communication contexts, a preference for lower-pitched voices regardless of gender."* Voice-AI assistant studies are the main exception — there higher pitch sometimes scored more trustworthy, but those are private-assistance contexts.
[PMC link](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11931160/)

**Baseline Hz ranges** (multiple sources): Adult male average ~116 Hz (range 85–155 Hz). Adult female average ~205 Hz (range 165–255 Hz). The "low-mid-pitched authoritative" target band: men ~95–120 Hz, women ~165–185 Hz.

**Voice of Authority (Journal of Nonverbal Behavior):** Professionals lower their F0 when giving expert advice — i.e., low pitch is the *behavior* of perceived experts, not just preferred *by* listeners.
[Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10919-019-00307-0)

> **Take:** target the bottom third of male F0 range, NOT a fake-deep "movie trailer voice" that registers as theatrical.

### 2.2 Pace / WPM

- **150–160 WPM is the comprehension+persuasion sweet spot.** NCVS-cited norm. Above 180 WPM = 17–25% drop in recall.
- **Persuasion specifically:** the 1976 Miller study found 195 WPM more persuasive than 102 WPM when listener disagreed; the 1991 follow-up showed 102 WPM more persuasive for pro-attitudinal messages. Judges scoring a hackathon submission are agnostic-to-skeptical, so faster (150–160) wins.
- **Steve Jobs Apple keynotes:** 145–155 WPM average. He deliberately dropped to **~90 WPM for the 180-word product intro** — the slow open is a textbook anticipation device.
- **Context tiering:** informative 130–140 WPM, persuasive 150–160 WPM, entertaining 160–170 WPM.

[Hyperbound — 150 WPM sales sweet spot](https://www.hyperbound.ai/blog/150-words-minute-sales-pace) · [Autoppt WPM guide](https://autoppt.com/blog/ideal-speaking-rate-wpm/) · [Speechmirror — Jobs analysis](https://speechmirror.space/speech-examples/apple-keynote-presentation-analysis)

> **Take:** 150 WPM body, 90–110 WPM cold-open and any "we then partnered with [F500 name]" moment.

### 2.3 Prosody — pitch variation, pauses, vocal flaws

**Monotone is the #1 credibility killer.** 74% of employees say slow monotone delivery is the top reason they disengage in presentations. Multiple studies link **greater pitch variation → greater perceived competence, charisma, character, sociability**.
[Persuasive power of prosodic features (ResearchGate)](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/329076788_Persuasive_power_of_prosodic_features) · [CIVA model — vocal affect in persuasion (Springer)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10919-021-00373-3)

**Pause length sweet spot:** mean comma pauses 0.38–0.67s, period pauses 0.81–1.24s, ratio ~1:2. Strategic pauses at clause boundaries elicit assents and signal "this matters." A 0.6–0.9s pause before a key claim is the standard rhetorical move (Churchill, Jobs).
[Frontiers — Pause Duration Influences Impressions](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.778018/full) · [Power of pausing in collaborative conversations (ScienceDirect)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0749597825000676)

**Credibility killers in corporate:**
- **Uptalk:** 85% of 700 UK bosses surveyed said uptalk indicates insecurity/weakness; 57% said it damages credibility.
- **Vocal fry (Anderson et al. 2014 PLOS One):** >80% of listeners said they'd be more likely to hire normal-voice speakers over vocal-fry speakers. Fry voices rated less competent, educated, trustworthy, attractive, hireable.
[PLOS — Vocal Fry & Labor Market](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0097506) · [Pacific Executives — speaking habits that undermine authority](https://pacificexecutives.com/insights/vocal-fry-uptalk-and-other-speaking-habits-that-undermine-authority-in-interviews)

> **Take:** high pitch variation, hard veto on uptalk and fry, 0.7s mean pause before stat drops.

### 2.4 Accent and dialect

- **Wang et al. (and HBR 2026):** U.S. participants trusted British accents more than Indian accents; "accent penalty" reduces perceived warmth/trust when accent isn't expected. American listeners associate British English with "intelligent, refined, well-spoken."
[HBR — Accent Penalty 2026](https://hbr.org/2026/03/research-how-the-accent-penalty-determines-who-gets-heard) · [Cornell HD Today — Accents We Trust](https://hdtoday.human.cornell.edu/2018/07/31/the-accents-we-trust/)
- **Frontiers 2024 — Accent + voice-AI assistant credibility:** Accent-mismatch with expected speaker context increases cognitive load and reduces perceived credibility. For US tech demos, US judges expect General American.
[Frontiers — accent + AI assistants](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/computer-science/articles/10.3389/fcomp.2024.1411414/full)

> **Take:** General American beats RP for this audience even though RP has a small "refinement" edge in absolute terms — the audience cost of unexpected accent > the refinement upside. Skip Australian, regional US, and any non-native English.

### 2.5 Gender

**Honest take: research is not clean on gender.** What the data actually says:

- **Female voiceovers test better in *some* settings:** Viewers reported trusting female narration more in a generic-viewer CXL study (66% preferred female VO). But this is consumer-explainer territory, not enterprise B2B.
[CXL — explainer video VO study](https://cxl.com/research-study/video-voice-over-study/)
- **Male voices dominate "authority/factual/neutral" framings,** especially in tech, finance, automotive product narration. This is a *practice* observation more than a hard psychology finding.
- **The Klofstad findings (lower-F0 wins) apply within-gender** — a low-pitched woman beats a high-pitched woman; a low-pitched man beats a high-pitched man. Cross-gender comparison is muddier.
- **Vocal fry penalty falls harder on women** (Anderson 2014), so a female pick has narrower margin for prosodic error.
- **Slack/Stripe/Linear/Apple/Google Cloud** product narrations in 2024–26 trend male baritone for hero product films, female mid-range for tutorials and onboarding. The hero spot for a hackathon submission is closer to the former.

> **Take:** Male baritone for GUARDIAN. A defensible second option is a low-mid female voice (~170 Hz, no fry, calm cadence) IF auditioning shows the male picks sound theatrical or AI-fake on this specific copy. Don't let the gender call become an ideology call — let the audition decide.

### 2.6 Real-world reference voices

- **Apple keynote-era voiceovers:** Jony Ive narrated most hero product films through 2019 (soft British baritone, ~110 Hz, deliberate pace). Post-Ive, Apple uses a rotating ensemble of in-house designers + occasional pro VO talent — no single named star.
- **Stripe Sessions / Google Cloud Next:** Both events use SAG-eligible US-market voice actors for hero films but credits aren't publicly listed. The *style* both converge on: calm American male baritone, 140–155 WPM, very low affect, generous pauses.
- **AWS re:Invent product films:** Same style profile.
- The consistent industry pattern is the strongest signal: enterprise B2B AI/cloud demos in 2024–26 default to American male baritone, 30s–40s, "documentary-news" register.

[Inc. — Apple designer on Jobs's rehearsal process](https://www.inc.com/carmine-gallo/a-long-time-apple-designer-reveals-steve-jobs-6-step-rehearsal-process-he-used-for-every-presentation.html)

### 2.7 AI-voice specific — passing the "AI slop" sniff test

**What gives AI voice away in 2025–26:**
1. **No breath sounds.** Real humans breathe; AI voices generate continuous sound without natural respiratory interruptions. Add `<break time="0.4s"/>` or audible inhales between long phrases.
2. **Statistically optimized pause placement** rather than human spontaneity. Vary pause lengths intentionally.
3. **Over-perfect pronunciation.** Slight micro-variations in vowel emphasis read as human.
4. **Monotone consistency** — same emphasis pattern on every line.
5. **Too-smooth HNR (harmonic-to-noise ratio).** A *touch* of HNR roughness reads more trustworthy to native English listeners per the 2025 Frontiers review.

## 4. SETTINGS RECOMMENDATIONS

For **Brian** (pick #1), using ElevenLabs Multilingual v2 or v3 Natural mode. Same settings work for George with minor tweaks.

```
stability:          0.55
similarity_boost:   0.80
style:              0.0
use_speaker_boost:  true
speaking_rate:      0.95   (v3 only; defaults to 1.0 — 0.95 = ~150 WPM target)
```

**Justifications (each from ElevenLabs official guidance + best-practice sources):**

- **stability 0.55** — ElevenLabs docs say 0.6–0.8 for audiobook/podcast (high stability = consistent). For a 3-minute persuasive demo we want *some* expressiveness, so we dip to 0.55. Too high = monotone (research-confirmed credibility killer). Too low = inconsistent across 12 segments and risks hallucinations. [ElevenLabs best practices](https://elevenlabs.io/docs/overview/capabilities/text-to-speech/best-practices) · [NeuraPlusAI 2026 settings guide](https://neuraplus-ai.github.io/blog/best-settings-for-elevenlabs-ai-voice-quality-improvement-2026.html)
- **similarity_boost 0.80** — official docs warn: "Clarity + Similarity Boost should generally stay between 75-90%. Pushing it to 100% can cause the AI to over-enunciate, creating an unnatural 'news anchor' effect." 0.80 is mid-range — captures Brian's character without going robotic.
- **style 0.0** — official docs: "it is recommended to keep this setting at 0 at all times." Higher style values cost latency and tend to caricature.
- **use_speaker_boost true** — preserves the deep-bass character of Brian that we specifically chose this voice for.
- **speaking_rate 0.95** — Brian's default cadence runs slightly fast for the comprehension+persuasion target band (150 WPM). Light slowdown to 0.95 lands us at ~150 WPM. Reserve 0.85 for the opening hook and any KPMG-style name-drop. Don't go below 0.7 (ElevenLabs minimum) and never above 1.05 for narration.

**For v3 specifically:** use **Natural** stability mode (not Robust — Robust ignores prosody/emotion direction; not Creative — Creative hallucinates over 12 segments). Natural is the documented sweet spot for corporate narration.

**SSML / audio-tag direction for AI-slop avoidance:**
- Insert `<break time="0.5s"/>` before every stat / KPMG / product name.
- Insert `<break time="0.7s"/>` after the cold-open hook line.
- Use punctuation aggressively — commas every 7–10 words to force micro-prosody.
- Avoid em-dashes in copy (they render flat in TTS) — use periods or semicolons.
- For v3, sparingly: `[calm]`, `[deliberate]`. Avoid `[excited]` or `[whispered]` — they read as performance and undercut authority.
