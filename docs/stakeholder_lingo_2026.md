# GUARDIAN stakeholder language for the 2026 demo

## Purpose

This file reframes stakeholder language so the demo is easy to understand for Google judges, Fortune 500 buyers, and general viewers, while keeping Google agent tooling terms exactly as they are: **A2A, CLI, SDK, ADK, Cloud Run, agent.json, mTLS**. The sustainability, audit, and conservation terms should be written in full on first mention so the story reads clearly to a human audience and still signals enterprise credibility.

## Naming principle

Keep the product and agent architecture precise; soften only the human-facing stakeholder language. In the demo, each short stakeholder label should map to a real-world role, department, or institution that exists in 2026.

## Use this framing

- **F500 sustainability buyer** = Chief Sustainability Officer, Head of Sustainability, or ESG Reporting Lead at a Fortune 500 or large multinational company responsible for nature-related disclosure, board reporting, and assurance readiness under the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive and the European Sustainability Reporting Standards, especially ESRS E4 Biodiversity and Ecosystems.
- **Sponsor Sustainability agent** = the corporate-facing agent that helps the F500 sustainability buyer prepare disclosures aligned with the Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures, support audit-ready evidence, and generate board-level reporting materials.
- **Park Authority** = the national park authority, reserve operator, or ranger operations command team responsible for field response and wildlife protection.
- **Park Service agent** = the field-response agent that coordinates dispatch information for ranger teams and incident handling inside the protected area.
- **Funder Reporter** = the program officer, impact lead, or reporting lead inside a conservation funder, wildlife NGO, or philanthropic program that needs proof that funded interventions created measurable conservation outcomes.
- **Neighbor Park** = an adjacent protected area or cross-border reserve partner that participates in wildlife corridor coordination and incident handoff.
- **Falsifier** = the adversarial review agent that challenges the recommended action before escalation, helping demonstrate rigor, disagreement handling, and evidence discipline in the system design.

## Terms to spell out on first mention

- **TNFD** = Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures.
- **CSRD** = Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive.
- **ESRS** = European Sustainability Reporting Standards.
- **ESRS E4** = European Sustainability Reporting Standard E4: Biodiversity and Ecosystems.
- **IUCN** = International Union for Conservation of Nature.
- **CITES** = Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora.
- **SHA-256** = Secure Hash Algorithm 256-bit, used here to express tamper-evident chain of custody for evidence.
- **Big Four audit firms** = Deloitte, PwC, EY, and KPMG.

## Demo voice

Use short language that sounds real in the room:

> GUARDIAN detects a possible poaching event in real time. Its agents verify the signal, challenge the recommendation, dispatch the park team, and create an evidence trail. The park authority gets faster action, the conservation funder gets measurable impact reporting, and the Fortune 500 sustainability buyer gets disclosure-ready proof for Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures and Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive reporting.

## What to avoid

- Avoid unexplained acronyms on screen.
- Avoid internal-sounding labels without a human translation.
- Avoid naming WWF, IUCN, or a specific park as the canonical stakeholder title unless it is clearly marked as an example, not the role itself.
- Avoid changing Google tooling language; the Google terms are part of the product credibility in this hackathon context.
